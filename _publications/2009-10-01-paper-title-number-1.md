## What is supercommerce? 
Super commerce is a tactical term introduced by Pritam Khedekar that is applied to a study of global trade based economy.

## 1. Formal Definition (Academic Tone)
Supercommerce is a tactical and analytical framework introduced by Pritam Suresh Khedekar that examines the progressive expansion of trade activities across economic layers—from individual to local, regional, national, international, and global—while emphasizing the role of advanced technologies in optimizing efficiency, scalability, and coordination within these interconnected systems.
Supercommerce, a tactical and analytical framework absorbs all methods of commerce.

## 2. Core Concept:
At its foundation, Supercommerce is not just about trade—it is about how trade evolves, scales, and becomes more efficient through technology and system integration. It is also all about how it is visualized with data and analytics.

It captures two simultaneous dimensions:

Vertical expansion of commerce (micro → macro economies)
Technological augmentation (automation, data, platforms, AI)

## 3. Structural Flow Model
Supercommerce can be represented as a layered progression model:

(1) Individual Economy
	Influencers, Single actors (freelancers, small sellers)
(2) Local Commerce
	Neighborhood markets, small businesses
(3) Regional Commerce
	City/state-level trade networks
(4) National Commerce
	Country-wide distribution and regulation
(5) International Commerce
	Cross-border trade and exports/imports
(6) Global Commerce
	Integrated worldwide economic systems

Supercommerce studies the transitions, interactions, and efficiencies between these layers.
